Ligatne

Population: 1 011 (2022)
Area: 7,1 km2
Distance from Riga: 71 km
Nearest towns: Sigulda, Cēsis, Vangaži

Sightseeing Objects

Cellar Cave Rocks of Ligatne

Rocky sandstone rocks were formed when water currents carried sandy, clayey rock particles. They slowly settled and compacted, becoming mighty formations of nature! The cellar caves of Ligatne were created by the ancient inhabitants with their own hands.

Ligatne Paper Mill

The history of producing paper in Līgatne dates back to 1815 when the first sheets of paper were produced of old fabric rags. The Paper Mill golden time was the end of 19th century, when it was granted permission to produce so called tsar’s paper – paper with watermark of tsarist Russia’s two-headed eagle. Nowadays Līgatne Paper Mill factory is not working anymore, but you have an opportunity to visit it with guide and see place, where paper were produced for almost 200 years.

Interesting Facts

1

Ligatne is one of the oldest paper production centers in Latvia. Paper was produced here in 1815 in the Paltmale manor flour mill adapted for paper production, but the paper factory was built a little later. After the construction of the paper factory, Līgatne began to develop into a settlement, which acquired city rights in 1993.

2

Ligatne is located in the territory of the Gauja National Park. Near Ligatne is the Ligatne Nature Trails - a forest massif with more than 5 km of tails and paths for pedestrians and cyclists, as well as horse riding, where you can observe the wild animals and birds characteristic of the Latvian fauna in aviaries.

3

There are winding river coasts with sandstone outcrops and rock-cut caves in Ligatne. It's a city with an abundance of colors in nature – rich green forests and meadows, shimmering golden, gray and reddish rocks and bluish-gray flow of the river.
