Kraslava

Population: 7 086 (2022)
Area: 8,5 km2
Distance of Riga: 266 km
Nationalities: 45% Latvians, 25% Russians, 17% Belarussians, 13% other nationalities
Nearest towns: Daugavpils, Dagda, Aglona

Sightseeing Objects

Kraslava St Ludwig Roman Catholic Church

An architectural monument of national importance, the pride of Krāslava city. The most vivid example of Latgalian Baroque architecture.

Address: Svētā Ludvika iela 3, Kraslava

Plater's Palace

Plater's Palace is located in the historical region of Latgale, in eastern Latvia. It is an 18th-century manor ensemble with main manor house and other manor buildings and a Baroque-style park.

Address: Pils iela 6, Kraslava

Enjoy Architecture of Ancient Houses!

There are many Russian-style residential buildings built at the beginning of the 20th century and later with interesting details such as many window panes, ornaments, specific coloring, etc. in Kraslava.

Interesting Facts

1

Archaeological excavations show that the coasts of the Daugava river in Kraslava were already inhabited in the 3rd millennium BC.

2

The most common variant - the name "Krāslava" comes from the Latgalian word "krāsls" - a chair, because the Daugava near Kraslava throws several arcs that resemble a chair in shape. The second explanation talks about the fact that the name of Kraslava comes from another Latgalian word "krāsla" - twilight.

3

Kraslava stands out for the fact that this city is multinational, as there are approx. 45% of the population are Latvians, 25% - Russians, 17% - Belarusians, 9% - Poles, 4% - residents of other nationalities.
