Riga

Population: 605 802 (2022)
Area: 304 km2
Nationalities: 46,6% Latvians, 34,6% Russians, 4,9% Ukrainians, 13,9% Others
Nearest cities: Jurmala, Salaspils, Kekava

Sightseeing Objects

The Old Town of Riga

The Old Town is the oldest part of the Riga, and — even though in its primordial state most of the buildings were made of wood — currently, there are a lot of architectural works remaining from the times of renaissance, baroque and medieval times in the middle of the unique and notable buildings in the style of Art Nouveau, especially the ones created by the locally and internationally well-known architect Mikhail Eisenstein.

Arcadia Park

One of the most beautiful and oldest landscaped gardens in Riga, located in a microdistrict called Tornakalns. The park was developed at different levels to diversify its landscape, thus providing for a variety of surprising views. The area around Māra's Pond was long a favourite place for recreation. Arcadia's winding paths, romantic atmosphere, little waterfalls and tiny bridges let city dwellers escape from their daily routine and dream of the pastoral. Although the festivals and concerts of yore are now mere echoes, the romance endures if you are looking for a quiet stroll.

Mezaparks

Mezaparks is one of most beautiful, green and prestige Riga's microdistricts, also it's a popular zone of recreation.

Riga Zoo

The Riga Zoo houses around 4000 animals of nearly 500 species.
