Madona

Population: 6 715 (2022)
Area: 10,4 km2
Distance from Riga: 165 km
Nationalities: 79% Latvians, 16% Russians, 1,8% Belarussians, 3,2% Others
Nearest towns: Cesvaine, Gulbene, Varaklani

Sightseeing Objects

Madona House of Culture

The building was once constructed as a Guards house (architect V. Vitands), which was opened in 1935. The relief of a soldier (sculptor K. Zemdega) above the entrance still reminds of the needs of that time. At that time, the Madona Guards House was the largest new building in Latvia outside Riga.

Adrress: Raiņa iela 12, Madona

Madona Prayer Chapel

The chapel is included in the tourist guide of the city of Madona, confirming that any person, regardless of denominational affiliation and time of day, is welcome here.

Is located at the intersection of Raiņa and Blaumaņa streets.

Saieta Square

The central square of Madona, which was a market square at the beginning of the 20th century. After extensive restoration, it has become a noteworthy urban object. On the northern side of the square are the city and county municipalities and Madona county TIC.

Interesting Facts

1

Madona is one of the highest cities in Latvia (122 - 193 m above sea level), its terrain difference is 71 m. It can be clearly seen when walking along Raiņa Street.

2

Madona is considered the winter capital, because the infrastructure of winter sports is developed in its surroundings, and the snow stays here the longest in spring.

3

The first art school in Latvia was estabilshed in Madona, in 1975.

4

The monument dedicated to Rudolfs Blaumanis (Latvian writer) in front of the House of Culture is the heaviest object in the collection of the Madona Museum, besides, it is not located in the museum premises, but in the open air. It's interesting that the appearance of R. Blaumanis on the monument is compared to the character of the cartoon "The Simpsons" Ned Flanders.
